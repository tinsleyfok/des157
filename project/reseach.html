These labs focus on different aspects such as operational energy, mobility, outdoor comfort, daylight and lifecycle. Architecture/operational energy: Urban Building Energy Model MIT sustainable design lab is grounding in architecture that develop design workflows, planning tools and metrics to evaluate the environmental performance of building and neighborhoods. For example, there is a project in Boston about energy efficiency strategies for buildings which basically focus on carbon reduction. The lab tries to elaborate an Urban Building Energy Models. The analysis begun with the Boston GIS (Geography Information System). This system combines data base like building geometry, parcel use and tax assessment data. Based on these data, the lab divides Boston into 48 buildings, 12 catalogs like residential or office and four-time period, such as wall construction, schedule, electric lighting and HVAC system. It also considers local weather. Base on the model, the city government can build a reduce-energy plan.

Urban Mobility: Sustainable Transport Sustainable transport is the broad of transport that is good for environmental and climate impacts. It can be improvement in fuel efficiency or vehicle emissions controls, or it also can be migrating transportation from fossil-based energy to new recourses. Nowadays, there are many project about sustainability or urban mobility. For example, like Tesla Model S or BMW i. These cars model are all environmental friendly and fully incorporate a sustainable and efficient urban mobile lifestyle.
